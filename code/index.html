Matlab Tutorial

As part of CELEST, I designed a Matlab tutorial for summer undergraduates with little or no programming experience. The tutorial consists of three files: CELEST_matlab.m, myFile.mat, and myFirstFunction.m (You can also download all three as a .rar file here ). You will need all three files to complete the tutorial.


To get started, open CELEST_matlab.m in the Matlab editor and read the comments carefully (Green text preceded by a percent sign % are comments). Copy and paste the lines that are not commented (black text) into the Matlab command window.


Computational Model of Visuomotor Reach Adaptation

As a teaching assistant for the class CN540 - Neural and Computational Models of Adaptive Movement Planning and Control, I created a final project assignment and a computational model inspired by Bullock et al. (1993) and Grosse-Wentrup and Vidal (2007).


The project challenges the student to understand how the brain learns to perform visually-guided reaches by integrating both motor and visual sensory feedback. In order to complete the assignment, the student must show understanding of the model by adding additional features to the model and by critiquing the added features.


References: Bullock, D., Grossberg, S., & Guenther, F. H. (1993). A self-organizing neural model of motor equivalent reaching and tool use by a multijoint arm. Journal of Cognitive Neuroscience, 5(4), 408-435.


Grosse-Wentrup, M., & Contreras-Vidal, J. L. (2007). The role of the striatum in adaptation learning: a computational model. Biological Cybernetics, 96(4), 377-388.
